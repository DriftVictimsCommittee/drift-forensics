# Reference #105

## Subject

Pre-incident Durable Nonce accounts creation anomaly

## Source URL

[https://github.com/DriftVictimsCommittee/drift-forensics/tree/main/nonce_check_test](https://github.com/DriftVictimsCommittee/drift-forensics/tree/main/nonce_check_test)

## Description

Forensic analysis of on-chain data reveals that four Durable Nonce accounts were created by the same initiator (`FMJnBkVpHj5JzN7w4XFysCwY931CYSYk1DsXzqNi7YPF`) within a short timeframe prior to the exploit. 

Critically, one of these accounts designated an active member of the Drift Security Council as its `Authority`. 

This serves as a clear, publicly visible Indicator of Compromise (IoC) that was present on-chain for over 39 hours before the multisig migration.

## Timeline & Visibility Window

The existence of this account created a critical warning window that was ignored during the subsequent security migration:

1.  **March 24, 01:22:06 UTC**: Suspicious nonce account `7s7s6saC5LHZoLyBXLM3pCjpWaA7meyQdP8NiH9ktAeC` created with Council Member `39JyWrdbVdRqjzw9yyEjxNtTbTKcTPLdtdCgbz7C7Aq8` as Authority.
2.  **March 25, 16:58:31 UTC**: Drift Protocol executes multisig migration, **retaining** `39JyWrdbVdRqjzw9yyEjxNtTbTKcTPLdtdCgbz7C7Aq8` as a council member in the new configuration.
3.  **April 01, 2026**: Exploit occurs. The retained council member `39JyWrdbVdRqjzw9yyEjxNtTbTKcTPLdtdCgbz7C7Aq8` uses this specific nonce account to sign the malicious governance transaction.

**Total Warning Window:** ~39 hours and 36 minutes.

## Consequences of Account Existence

The presence of this nonce account had two critical implications:

1.  **Proof of Prior Compromise:** The creation of a nonce account by a third party (`FMJnBkVpHj5JzN7w4XFysCwY931CYSYk1DsXzqNi7YPF`) for a council member (`39JyWrdbVdRqjzw9yyEjxNtTbTKcTPLdtdCgbz7C7Aq8`) implies that the attacker already had control over the council member's signing capability or was preparing it for offline execution. 

2.  **Bypass of Recent Blockhash:** By using a durable nonce, the attacker could sign the transaction at any time after creation, decoupling the signing event from the execution event.

## Methods for Detection

As demonstrated in [Ref-202](https://github.com/DriftVictimsCommittee/drift-forensics/blob/main/refs/ref-202.md), detecting such anomalies requires only a basic RPC query against the System Program. 

A simple check for any nonce account where the `Authority` matches a council member's public key would have flagged this account immediately.
